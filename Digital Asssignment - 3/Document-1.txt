Andhra Pradesh was once a major Buddhist pilgrimage site in the country and a Buddhist learning center, which can be seen in many sites in the state in the form of monastery ruins, chaityas and stupas. Locally mined diamonds include the Koh-i-Noor from Kollur Mine. It is also a major producer of rice known as the "rice bowl of India". Its official language is Telugu; one of the classical languages of India, the fourth most spoken language in India and the 11th-most spoken language in the world. Andhra Pradesh's second official language is Urdu.
